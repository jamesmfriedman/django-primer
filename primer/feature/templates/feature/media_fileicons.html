File Icons nothing but CSS

I hit a dead end on the web when trying to find some simple vector file icons. After some head scratching, it occured to me that I could make them with just CSS. In an effort to keep the markup clean and simple, they rely on gratuitous use of the :before and :after psuedo elements. They are built entirely on em units so you can scale them by adjusting the element's or its parent's font-size. Surprisingly, they work just fine in IE8, although they will be missing some of the bells and whistles of CSS3.

<!-- most common extensions are supported. Just append it to the class fileicon- -->
<div class="fileicon"></div>
<div class="fileicon-txt"><label>TXT</label></div>

Interactive with Labels

This resembles more of a filesystem style interaction with the icons and also allows you to add a label for the filename. You can add a class of active to the .fileicon-wrapper element to get a selected state.
